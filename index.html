About

Undergraduate Research Assistant at Information Processing and Transmission Lab, NUST.

I started my research in machine learnign at TUKL Deep Learning Lab, NUST. In Summer 2021', I worked on TabAug, a tabular augmentation technique for table detection and information extraction. Then, my project trajectory changed to Vision Transformers, and I implemented a novel augmentation technique for Vision Transformers in the domain of table detection. This work was subsequently accepted at ICDAR 2023 USA.

In Summer 2022', I interned at Machine Vision and Intelligence Lab, NUST. I worked on a project titled "Orientation aware weapon detection". This encompassed the meticulous manual annotation of six thousand images based on weapon orientation. Subsequently, I undertook extensive data preprocessing and cleaning to ensure the dataset's readiness for input into the model. Notably, this project culminated as the final design thesis for a Master's student within the lab.

In Summer 2023', My interest inclined more towards wireless communications. I undertook my senior design thesis in this domain and worked on "Deep Reinforcement Learning for Wireless Resource Allocation for 6G Networks". I have published two research papers at esteemed IEEE Venues of IEEE Globecom 2023 and IEEE Communication Letters 2023.

As for my future plans, I am looking forward to pursuing a Ph.D in Wireless Communications and Machine Learning from US.

My research interests lie in wireless communications and integrating modern knowledge of machine learning to wirless systems. I am open to workin on interesting problems within and beyond this domain. Feel Free to reach me out.
